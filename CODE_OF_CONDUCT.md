# Code of Conduct

## Our values

The development of DHARMa and all associated activities — including discussions on the issue tracker, pull request reviews, and interactions in any other forum related to this project — is grounded in the following values:

**Scientific integrity.** DHARMa is a tool used in ecological and statistical research. We are committed to maintaining correctness, transparency, and reproducibility in the package's methods and documentation. Issues, corrections, and improvements are always welcome.

**Free and open knowledge.** DHARMa is free software and is developed in the spirit of open science. We believe that scientific tools should be openly accessible and that knowledge should be shared freely.

**Mutual respect.** All participants in this project are expected to engage respectfully and in good faith, regardless of background, level of expertise, or point of view. Constructive disagreement is welcome; personal attacks, dismissive language, and bad-faith engagement are not.

## Human authorship

Interactions in this repository — including issues, comments, and pull requests — are expected to originate from human contributors who take personal responsibility for their content. Submissions generated autonomously by AI agents or bots are not appropriate and will be closed without review. See [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md) for details on the policy regarding AI-generated pull requests.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies within all spaces associated with DHARMa, including GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other communication channels used for the project.

## Reporting

If you observe behavior that violates these values, please contact the maintainer directly via email (available on the [GitHub profile](https://github.com/florianhartig)). Reports will be handled with discretion.
